Video

I spend a lot of time in class and reading books to learn about programming, but I spend even more time watching the plethora of incredible tutorial style videos from the myriad of e-education sites that have sprung up over the last 5 years. Below is a small sampling of some of my favourites

Link Content
Lynda.com Professional video tutorials site. Paid subscription model. This is one of the go to sites for great video tutorials covering a very wide range of tech topics from using a Mac to programming Java and almost everything in between.
Udemy User / Teacher submitted tutorials with tons of content - free and paid model. You take the good with the bad here but for the most part there is a ton of high quality learning content on this site.
Code School Paid subscription model. This is a site built on learning to code interactively. It is a fantastic way to learn as it provides instant feedback as you progress through the levels of the courses. What they lack in quantity of courses they more than make up for in quality.
iTunes U Free education at your fingertips. The iTunes U application for iOS, and also available via the iTunes application, offers access to courses from some of the most respected educational institutes on the planet. Each of the hundreds of universities has essentially set up live taping in the lectures for any given course and offers the recordings to the masses through the iTunes U interface. This is as close as some of us may ever get to attending courses at MIT or Stanford. While you can't get educational credits through iTunes you it is still as though were right there attending the classes in person. If you love to learn, this is the place for you
